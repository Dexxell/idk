Science Facts

1. The Speed of Light

The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately 299,792,458 meters per second. This is the fastest speed at which energy or information can travel in the universe.

2. Human DNA

History Facts

1. The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China, one of the largest construction projects ever completed, stretches over 13,000 miles and was built over several centuries to protect against invasions.

2. The Rosetta Stone

The Rosetta Stone was discovered in 1799 and played a crucial role in deciphering Egyptian hieroglyphs, providing insight into ancient Egyptian culture and language.

3. The Printing Press

Johannes Gutenberg's invention of the printing press in the mid-15th century revolutionized the spread of knowledge, making books and information more accessible to the masses.

Technology Facts

1. The First Computer

The first electronic general-purpose computer, ENIAC, was developed in 1945 and occupied 1,800 square feet. It could perform 5,000 additions per second.

2. The Internet's Growth

In 1993, the World Wide Web had only 130 websites. Today, there are over 1.5 billion websites, illustrating the massive growth and importance of the internet.

3. The First Smartphone

The first smartphone, IBM's Simon, was introduced in 1994. It featured a touch screen, email capabilities, and even apps, paving the way for modern smartphones.
